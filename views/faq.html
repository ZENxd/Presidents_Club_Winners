US Winners:

Per IRS regulations, certain expenses associated with your trip are not considered business expenses and will be added as taxable income to you. Examples of these expenses are gifts you or your guest receive, the cost of the various hotel activities you select, and the cost of renting your formal wear. The value of these expenses will be added to your paycheck as taxable gross income in April, 2015. You will also receive a bonus equal to 75% of the taxable gross amount to help offset the additional tax liability you will incur. If you have any questions concerning this procedure, please contact Amber Colandone – US Country HR, 408/Telnet 553-3440 or via email at amber_colandone@agilent.com

What should I wear to the President’s Club?

Oahu is generally informal. Attire for the program is casual resort wear, with the exception of the Awards Banquet, which is formal/black tie (tuxedos for the gentlemen and short or long evening dresses for the ladies). Native costumes from your home country are encouraged for this evening. Lightweight cotton and natural fabrics are recommended clothing for the type of climate on Oahu. A jacket or sweater is advisable for the outdoor evening activities. Bathing suits are acceptable only at the beaches and pool; the Kahala Hotel & Resort requests that appropriate attire (shirts and shoes) be worn while on the property.

What is the time zone on Oahu?

Time Difference: Hawaii’s time zone is 10 hours behind Greenwich Meridian Time. When it is noon on Monday in Hawaii, it is:
2:00 pm Monday in California
9:00 pm Monday in London
5:00 am Tuesday in Hong Kong
6:00 am Tuesday in Japan and Korea
