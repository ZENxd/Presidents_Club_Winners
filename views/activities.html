Hawai'i is known as one of the world's great whale watch locations, famous for the majestic humpback whales that migrate here for the winter and spring calving season. The captain and crew are trained naturalists and offer fascinating whale and dolphin facts during the cruise.

SURF & CANOE WITH WAIKIKI BEACH BOYS

Exertion Level:

Dates:
Friday, March 4, 2016

The original Waikīkī beach boys of the 1920’s were water sports instructors who taught visitors how to surf, catch waves in an outrigger canoe, and enjoy the Hawaiian culture. They were known for their charm and their love of Hawai`i, which they instilled in the tourists they befriended. As a result, the beach boys became a symbol of aloha, and helped draw tourists to the islands. Today, surfing lessons and canoe rides are still conducted by these ambassadors of aloha.

You’ll be escorted to the famed surfing spots of “Canoes” or “Baby Royal” where Hawai`i’s legend, Duke Kahanamoku, surfed on his heavy koa board. With your instructors leading the way, you’ll feel the “rush” as you paddle to catch a wave. Experience the joy and freedom of riding it all the way in, or, take a ride in a real Hawaiian outrigger canoe.

After your fun in the sun, relax in the shade at Duke’s Canoe Club. Enjoy a refreshing tropical mai tai and island style lunch as you watch beach-goers trying their hand at the very skills you’ve just mastered!

Board a specially-designed 5-million dollar underwater viewing vessel. You 'll descend to depths of over 100 feet where you'll stay submerged for approximately 40 minutes! Exertion Level:

The Arizona Memorial and Battleship Missouri are visual reminders of both the triumphs and sacrifices we faced in World War II. For Hawai'i, the war began when Pearl Harbor was bombed. Your visit to the Arizona Memorial will take you back in time. Throughout that dark day, there were many acts of heroism that we can still celebrate.

Fast forward to the teak decks of the big Battleship Missouri. Here, the war ended when the Japanese signed the surrender documents. We had secured our place once again as the protector of our country's waterways and the world's oceans. By visiting both the memorial and the battleship, you'll have a complete picture of the era.

LITTLE CIRCLE ISLAND TOUR

Exertion Level:

Dates:
Thursday, March 3, 2016

O'ahu's western shore is a sunny playground dotted with little oases of green. As you head east, the vistas change with some areas lending a more "jungle" feel.

But before you hit the tree line, you'll cruise along the southeastern coast. One of her spectacular gems is the circular-shaped shore of Hanauma. This bay was once a volcanic crater, which has now become a marine preserve.

Just down the coast, you'll visit Halona. This blowhole is a natural occurrence formed by molten lava tubes from volcanic eruptions thousands of years ago. The lava tubes run to the ocean and, when the surf is right. the blowhole shoots water up to 30 feet in the air. The larger the waves, the larger the spray!

The drive continues through the beachside town of Waimanalo and lastly a stop at the Pali Lookout. A site of deep historical significance, "Poli" meaning 'cliff' in Hawaiian, is the site of the Battle of Nuuanu. This fierce battle claimed hundreds of soldiers' lives, many of which were forced off of the Pali 's sheer cliffs.

GOLF AND THE ROYAL HAWAIIAN COUNTRY CLUB

Exertion Level:

Dates:
Thursday, March 3, 2016

The Royal Hawaiian Country Club has 18 holes, par 72 and 6,595 yards. Located in the back of beautiful Maunawili Valley on the windward side of O'ahu, the Royal Hawaiian Country Club is the first course in Hawai'i designed by Perry Dye and his famous brother, Pete - one of the most accomplished golf architects in America. The Dye brothers incorporated many of their trademark design features, including a variety of forced carries off the tee, bunkers bordered by railroad ties, par-3's over water to island greens, and fairways that fall off steeply into bunkers and ravines.

The Royal Hawaiian Country Club is set in awesome natural surroundings beneath the deep green folds of the Ko'olau Mountains. The front nine plays across the lower slopes of Mount Olomana, while the back nine wends its way through a tropical rainforest with dramatic changes in elevation. Locals refer to it as "Jurassic Park" because there are few signs of civilization, only the surrounding forest and the mountain peaks overhead. Don't let the beauty consume you - the Royal Hawaiian Country Club is a challenging high risk/high rewards course.
