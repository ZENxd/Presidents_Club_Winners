MEASUREMENTS

* Height

Height is required.

* Weight

Weight is required.

Measure from top of head to the floor without shoes for height.

* waist

Waist is required.

Tuxedo trousers have a longer rise than most trousers; therefore they must be worn higher than the waist. To find the proper fit, measure just above the hipbone across the navel. Make sure there is no bulky material around the waist. Insert a finger between the tape and the body to insure proper ease.

* Out Seam

Out Seam is required.

Stand straight with eyes looking forward. Draw an imaginary line horizontal from the navel to just above the hips. Extend the tape down the side of the leg to the outside arch of the shoe. This measurement gives you the length of the trousers as well as a guide for coat length. Make sure you are wearing shoes you will be wearing for the event, or a close approximation.

* Coat Insleeve

Coat Insleeve is required.

This is a measurement of the coat itself. It is the distance between the cuff of the coat sleeve to the underarm following the inside seam of the sleeve. If you should need the sleeves altered from the standard lengths, indicate it with your request.

* Hip

Hip is required.

This is often overlooked, but very important measurement. Even though the waist may measure a certain size, the hips must be able to fit into the trouser without pulling. Remove any bulky articles from your pockets. Place the tape directly over the pants around the largest part of the seat.

* Chest

Chest is required.

This measurement is taken with the coat off, around the chest. Place the tape well up under the arms, over the shoulder blades, and over the chest in front. Insert finger between the tape and the body to insure proper ease. For a true measurement make sure you are not flexing your chest and your arms are down at your sides.

* Neck

Neck is required.

Measure the collar unbuttoned. Place the tape low on the neck where the shirt collar normally falls. Allow a finger space between the neck and the tape. Make sure this measurement is in proportion to the other measurements.

* Sleeve

Sleeve is required.

With the arm straight down at your side, place tape at the base of the neck in back. Extend tape across the top of the shoulder, and down the arm to a point about one inch below the wrist bone. This measurement is for the shirt sleeve length which can also help to determine the coat length.

TUXEDO RENTAL FAQ

Here are some commonly asked questions regarding dress attire for the gala event:

Q: WHAT IS A TUXEDO?
It’s a formal black suit.

Q: WHO SHOULD WEAR A TUXEDO?
Typically men wear a tuxedo.

Q: WHAT DOES A TUXEDO INCLUDE?
Shirt, tie, pants, and shoes.  You must provide your own socks.

Q: SHOULD I HAVE MY MALE GUEST WEAR A TUXEDO, AND IF SO, SHOULD HE FILL OUT THE FORM?
Yes and yes.

Q: WHO PAYS FOR THE TUXEDO RENTAL?
The cost of the tuxedo rental is directly billed to Agilent.

Q: WHERE DO I PICK UP MY TUXEDO?
At the Kahala Hotel.  After you’ve arrived, you’ll go to a designated room at the Kahala to pick up your tuxedo.

Q: WHAT IF MY TUXEDO DOESN’T FIT?
The tuxedo company will be on site at the Kahala and they will make any alternations needed.

Q: MAY I WEAR MY OWN TUXEDO?
Of course.  Just send me an email and let me know.

Q: IS NATIVE DRESS FROM MY HOME COUNTRY ACCEPTABLE?
Yes!  Native dress is encouraged for this evening.

Q: WHAT TYPE OF "FORMAL" WEAR IS RECOMMENDED FOR WOMEN?
A short cocktail dress or a long gown is considered formal dressing for women (see examples below)
